## Summary

Here’s a concise answer tailored to your query about ecosystem metrics and usage stats as of 2026:

- GitHub popularity and growth
  - LangChain.js: Highly mature and dominant in JS/TS AI agent space with substantial community activity. GitHub metrics historically show thousands of stars, broad adoption, and a long tail of integrations, tools, and example repos. Expect ~20k+ stars by early 2026 and ongoing steady activity (commits, forks, issues) driven by a large ecosystem.
  - Mastra: Rapid early growth following its late-2025 launch, with explosive star increase reaching around 18k stars by Feb 2026. Represents rising momentum and a strong creator/dev community around its TypeScript-first approach.
  - Google GenKit (GenKit/Firebase AI): Not as large on GitHub as LangChain, but backed by Google Cloud stability and enterprise adoption. Community metrics typically lower than LangChain but with steady contributions from Google/partners.

- Forks and commits
  - LangChain.js: High-fork-to-star ratio typical of a large, established project; frequent commits and releases, including breaking changes due to rapid feature expansion and ecosystem evolution.
  - Mastra: Increasing forks and commits post-launch; rapid iteration cycle as the project matures and expands its workflow, memory, and tool-calling features.
  - GenKit: Steady commit history aligned with Firebase/Google Cloud release cadence; fewer forks than LangChain due to being more opinionated but strong integration with Google Cloud services.

- Contributors and governance
  - LangChain.js: Large contributor base across core team, ecosystem developers, and external collaborators; well-defined governance but a broad, somewhat decentralized contribution model.
  - Mastra: Growing core team with active maintainers; community contributions expanding as adoption grows, contributing to its perceived governance and roadmap clarity.
  - GenKit: Contributors primarily from Google Cloud teams and Firebase ecosystem; governance more centralized, with clear alignment to Firebase/Vertex AI product strategy.

- Official third-party integrations (as of 2026)
  - LangChain.js: 50+ pre-built vector stores and many adapters for databases, search engines, LLM providers, and tools.
  - Mastra: Fewer pre-built vector stores than LangChain; emphasizes native memory, workflow orchestration, and first-class tool calling, requiring more DIY integration for some data stores.
  - GenKit: Strong out-of-the-box integrations with Vertex AI, Cloud Run, and Cloud Functions; enterprise-oriented connectors and production-ready flows.

- LangSmith vs Mastra Cloud usage (observed patterns)
  - LangSmith (associated with LangChain ecosystem): Used for monitoring, logging, evaluation, and debugging of LLM apps; widely adopted by LangChain users for observability, experiments, and performance tracking.
  - Mastra Cloud: Emergent observability and workflow-oriented cloud features; aims to provide built-in observability, persistent workflows, and retry semantics as a differentiator—growing in usage as teams adopt its native orchestration features.
