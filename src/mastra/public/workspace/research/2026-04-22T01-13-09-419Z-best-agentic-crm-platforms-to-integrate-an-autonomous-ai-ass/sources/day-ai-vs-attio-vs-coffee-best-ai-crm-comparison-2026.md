## Summary

Here’s a concise, user-focused summary addressing your query about emerging AI-native CRMs in 2026 that expose agent APIs and orchestration primitives, including integration options, tradeoffs, and SMB suitability, drawn from the Coffee.ai Day.ai vs Attio vs Coffee comparison:

- Core takeaway: Coffee positions itself as an agent-driven CRM with proactive automation across the full sales workflow, including auto data entry, meeting intelligence, and post-call follow-ups. Day.ai emphasizes meeting intelligence but offers weaker CRM core features. Attio provides strong customization and flexible workflows but relies more on manual data entry than Coffee.
- Agent APIs and orchestration primitives (as described): Coffee promotes an end-to-end agent that auto-creates contacts/companies from emails and calendars, prepares briefings, joins calls for transcription, and auto-generates follow-ups. This approach implies exposed orchestration capabilities around meeting lifecycle automation and data creation within the CRM. Day.ai and Attio are portrayed as less aggressive in agent-driven orchestration, with Day.ai focusing on meeting summaries and Attio on flexible workflows.
- Integration options:
  - Coffee: Strong integration footprint, described as a “Salesforce companion mode” and broad automation across the workflow. Suitable for teams that want deep, agent-driven automation across data entry, meeting notes, and post-call activities.
  - Day.ai: Integrations are narrower, with emphasis on meeting intelligence rather than full CRM workflows.
  - Attio: Offers customizable UI and workflows with standard APIs; still requires manual data entry for full data completeness.
- Tradeoffs:
  - Coffee:
    - Pros: Highest auto data entry capability (9.5/10), proactive agent-driven automation, end-to-end meeting lifecycle support, SOC 2 compliance, reduces 8–12 hours of busywork weekly.
    - Cons: May require adherence to a more prescriptive agent-driven model; potential learning curve for teams moving from traditional CRM workflows.
  - Day.ai:
    - Pros: Strong meeting intelligence (8/10) and lightweight automation.
    - Cons: Weak core CRM features (pipeline management, structured deal tracking), limiting long-term sales process visibility.
  - Attio:
    - Pros: Excellent UI customization and flexible data models; strong workflow configurability.
    - Cons: Still relies on manual data entry to maintain data quality, which reduces automation gains.
- Suitability for SMBs (small- to mid-sized businesses):
  - Coffee: Most SMB-ready for those seeking immediate productivity gains via agent automation and minimal manual data maintenance. Best for teams with heavy meeting-based selling and preferring an integrated, agent-led workflow.
  - Day.ai: Might suit SMBs that prioritize meeting intelligence and are comfortable building out their own CRM processes with limited automation.
  - Attio: Appealing to SMBs that need highly customized data models and UI but are willing to invest in data hygiene and manual entry to realize flexible workflows.
- Bottom line for 2026 landscape:
  - The AI-native CRM space is trending toward agent-driven platforms that automate data entry, meeting workflows, and post-call activities.
