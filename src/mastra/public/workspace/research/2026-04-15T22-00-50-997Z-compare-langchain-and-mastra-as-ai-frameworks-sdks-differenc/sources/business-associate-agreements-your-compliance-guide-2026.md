- Key definitions:
  - BAA (Business Associate Agreement): A legally binding contract between a HIPAA Covered Entity and a third party (business associate) that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI. Under 45 CFR 164.502(e) and 164.504(e), BAAs are mandatory, not optional.
  - BAA purpose: Clarify responsibilities, ensure PHI protection, and close potential loopholes from verbal assurances.

- Why BAAs/DPAs matter for enterprises:
  - Ensure regulatory compliance when vendors handle PHI.
  - Define roles, responsibilities, safeguards, breach notifications, and liability.
  - Provide a documented risk management framework for outsourced or cloud-based processing.

- Practical takeaways for 2026 enterprise needs:
  - When engaging vendors handling PHI, implement a BAA that explicitly assigns responsibilities for: access controls, data security, breach notification timelines, data retention/deletion, subprocessor management, and incident response.
  - Use a DPA in addition to BAAs to define data processing terms if the vendor acts as a data processor on behalf of the covered entity.
