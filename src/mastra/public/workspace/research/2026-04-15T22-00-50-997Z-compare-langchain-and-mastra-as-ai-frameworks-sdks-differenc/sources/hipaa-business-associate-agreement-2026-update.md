## Summary

Summary:

- The page explains what a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (BAA) is: a contract between a HIPAA-covered entity and a business or individual that handles PHI on behalf of the entity, defining permitted PHI uses/disclosures and responsibilities for privacy and security.
- Key components of a compliant BAA:
  - Permitted uses and disclosures of PHI by the business associate (and any subcontractors).
  - How the business associate will support patients’ Privacy Rule rights.
  - Security measures and breach notification requirements to protect PHI.
  - Responsibilities of both parties to maintain confidentiality and PHI integrity.
- Outsourcing and third-party involvement:
  - When a covered entity outsources PHI-related functions to a third party, a BAA is required before PHI is disclosed.
  - If the business associate subcontracts to another entity (e.g., cloud providers), a downstream BAA is needed to ensure chain-of-custody and HIPAA compliance with PHI handling.
- Subcontractors:
  - Subcontractors that handle PHI must also comply with HIPAA, necessitating additional BAAs with the cloud/service providers involved.
- Training:
  - HIPAA training is emphasized for both entities and business associates to address HIPAA challenges, including AI tool usage and ongoing compliance.
- Resources:
  - A downloadable BAA template is provided (Word document) to help establish standard terms for permissible PHI use/disclosure, privacy rights support, and security obligations.
- Relevance to enterprise customers (Mastra-specific lens):
  - For enterprise buyers like Mastra, the material underlines the importance of a robust, 2026-ready BAA framework that covers third-party (subcontractor) risk, chain-of-custody for PHI, clear data processing terms, security controls, breach notification, and comprehensive training requirements.
- Practical takeaways for enterprise DPA/BAA programs:
  - Use or customize a comprehensive BAA template that aligns with 2026 HIPAA Omnibus expectations.
  - Ensure downstream BAAs with all subcontractors (e.g., cloud providers) are in place.
  - Include explicit PHI use limitations, data retention/destruction terms, access controls, and breach notification timelines.
  - Implement ongoing HIPAA training specific to business associates and subcontractors, with tracking and certification.
  - Attach or reference applicable security addenda and data processing addenda (DPAs) where applicable.
