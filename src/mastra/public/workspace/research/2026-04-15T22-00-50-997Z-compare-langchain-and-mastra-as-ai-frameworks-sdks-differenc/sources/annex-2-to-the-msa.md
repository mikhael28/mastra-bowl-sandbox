# ANNEX №2 to the MSA
- URL: https://maestra.io/documents/annex-2-msa/version-6-0/
- Query: Mastra Data Processing Addendum (DPA) and HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (BAA) documentation for enterprise customers 2026
## Summary

Summary:

- Document: Annex №2 to the Master Service Agreement (PDPA) — Personal Data Processing Agreement — US
- Parties: Customer (data Controller) and Contractor (Maestra) as Processor
- Scope: Governs processing of Personal Data required to provide Maestra Service; annex is part of the MSA
- Key obligations:
  - Processing limited to what is necessary for service provision and the Agreement
  - Processing conducted fairly and lawfully under US privacy law and Customer requests
- Sub-processors:
  - Contractor may engage Mindbox/Maestra affiliates and other service providers as Sub-processors
  - Customer-authorized; list available in the Privacy Policy or on request
  - Contractor remains fully liable for Sub-processor performance
- Security:
  - Implemented technical/organizational measures to protect Personal Data
  - No unnecessary access to Personal Data beyond what is required for service provisioning
- Data breach:
  - Immediate notification to Customer of actual or suspected breaches
  - Provide information needed for DPAs and data subject notifications upon request
- Data subject requests:
  - Customer has access to systems processing their data; if Customer lacks access, Contractor assists with necessary data to respond to access/rectification/erasure/restriction requests
- Confidentiality:
  - Personal Data treated as confidential; staff/third parties sign confidentiality agreements
  - Disclosure only as legally required; limited disclosure
  - Public authority requests: Contractor to inform Customer promptly

Relevance for enterprise customers seeking DPA/BAA in 2026:
- The cited Annex II covers general US-privacy-aligned data processing terms, subprocessors, security measures, breach notifications, and data subject rights within Maestra’s MSA framework.
- There is no explicit HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (BAA) or HIPAA-specific terms in this document. If HIPAA/BAA compliance is required, you should:
  - Verify whether Maestra offers a HIPAA-compliant BAA or an enterprise addendum with HIPAA terms.
  - Obtain a HIPAA-specific BAA detailing:
    - Covered Entities/Business Associates, permitted disclosures, and minimum necessary standard
    - Safeguards, incident response, breach notification timelines (e.g., within 60 days or applicable state/federal requirements)
    - Subcontractor requirements and business associate subcontractor controls
    - Privacy and security controls: access controls, encryption, audit rights
    - Data retention, deletion, and return/destruction of PHI
  - Ensure alignment with HIPAA definitions (PHI, ePHI, BA, etc.) and breach notification requirements.

If your goal is a combined DPA + HIPAA BAA for enterprise customers (2026), you’ll likely need:
- A HIPAA-specific BAA document or an enterprise data protection addendum that references the DPA and includes HIPAA terms
- Cross-reference to privacy policy and subprocessor list, ensuring PHI handling details are explicit
- Custom clauses for business associates, security rule compliance, breach timelines, and cooperation
