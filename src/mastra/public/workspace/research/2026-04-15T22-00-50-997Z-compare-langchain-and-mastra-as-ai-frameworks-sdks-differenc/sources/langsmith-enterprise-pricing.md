# LangSmith Enterprise Pricing
- URL: https://agentsapis.com/langsmith-pricing/enterprise-pricing/
- Query: LangChain / LangSmith enterprise features, observability and governance pages, SLA and compliance documentation
## Summary

Summary:

- LangSmith Enterprise is a custom, non-public per-seat plan designed for organizations with advanced hosting, security, and support needs. It’s not a simple self-serve tier.
- Key elements typically included in Enterprise:
  - Advanced hosting options, including hybrid or self-hosted deployments to keep data in your VPC.
  - Custom SSO and RBAC, often with centralized identity management and auditing.
  - Contracted support with an SLA, defined response times, and escalation paths.
  - Access to deployed engineering support, architectural guidance, and team trainings.
  - Custom workspaces, seat models (potentially negotiated), and scale-focused Agent Builder packages.
  - Annual invoicing and governance features aligned with procurement processes.
- What “custom” means in practice:
  - You work with LangSmith sales to define requirements, usage tiers (e.g., trace ingestion, retention), hosting, and security needs, resulting in a tailored quote.
  - Pricing is not linear; it reflects negotiated usage, hosting, and support levels rather than standard per-seat rates.
- When Enterprise is a good fit:
  - You require data governance, advanced access control, and enterprise-grade security/compliance.
  - You need data residency controls, hybrid/self-hosted options, and a formal support SLA.
  - Your org needs architectural guidance, trainings, and custom Agent Builder capabilities at scale.
- When it may not be necessary:
  - Small teams with straightforward access control and standard cloud hosting.
  - You’re comfortable with standard cloud hosting, self-serve billing, and basic support (non-SLA).
- Important note:
  - Enterprise terms vary by organization size, security requirements, region, and usage. Always verify the latest details on LangSmith’s official pricing page and with LangChain sales before budgeting or production use.

If you’re comparing to observability, governance, and SLA docs, Enterprise aligns with: contracted governance, enhanced security posture, centralized administration, and enterprise-grade support for LangSmith usage (trace retention, deployment runs, and Agent Builder at scale).
